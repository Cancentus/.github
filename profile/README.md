## Our vision

At Cancentus, we see toxicity to healthy tissue as the central obstacle to effective cancer treatment. Our work is focused on improving the therapeutic index, the balance between how well a treatment works and how safe it is for the patient.

We build our therapeutics around highly selective targets so that we can:

- **Kill cancer cells selectively** by hitting pathways whose inhibition is lethal to tumors while leaving healthy cells intact.
- **Keep treatments tolerable** so that patients can stay on therapy.
- **Give clinicians confidence** through the precision needed to prescribe with assurance.

## Our scientific approach

Our strategy centers on exploiting oncogene-induced DNA replication stress, a vulnerability that sets cancer cells apart from normal ones. By going after the pathways tumors rely on to cope with that stress, we can eliminate malignant cells while sparing healthy tissue. It is a shift away from broadly cytotoxic approaches and toward precision therapeutics that target the weaknesses created by oncogenic transformation.

We focus on novel targets and methods rather than "me-too" programs, and we embrace the complexity that genuinely effective treatments require while staying mindful of development and clinical hurdles.

## Programs

- **ADCs with a novel payload** — A cancer-selective antibody-drug conjugate payload class that exploits DNA replication stress, paired with a proprietary linker for selectivity, broad efficacy, and a path to overcoming resistance.
- **Small-molecule helicase inhibitor** — An under-studied DNA helicase identified as synthetic lethal with oncogene-induced replication stress, currently in lead optimization.
- **Synergistic combination therapy** — A novel combination of anti-cancer agents validated across preclinical xenograft models, with a defined path toward clinical translation.

### Therapeutic areas

We concentrate on two cancers united by oncogene-induced replication stress: **HER2+ breast cancer** and **ERG+ prostate cancer**.
